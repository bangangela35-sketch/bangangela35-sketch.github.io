2025 – The Spark

In December I decided to fill out the information for Tech-Moms. I knew that I wanted to go back to school, and Tech-Moms showed up on my Facebook feed. This is when I signed up for Tech-Moms Cohort: Foundational Program. This is where I began my journey into Tech. In this 9 week program, I learned the basics of coding. I learned HTML, CSS, and intro to JavaScript. Tech-Moms also brought different opportuntines for myself and I hope I can show you what I have been up too.

January 2026 – First Steps

On January 17th 2026, I started my first day back into a classroom setting as a Mom of 6. Then I started to learn these basic coding skills: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This would lead me to build a website for my final project. Also in this month, I learned about Networking, LinkedIn, Guest Speakers, Jobs in Tech and what Tech Week was. This is where my journey started to begin!

Febuary 2026 – Growing Skills

I started out with Febuary with a NetWorking event with Tech-Moms and Womens Tech Council. This event was a 3x3 NetWorking event. In this event, I truly got my eyes on how Networking works and how much power Women have in the Tech world. In this event I learned how to use my LinkedIn profile and start my Tech conncetions. I learned how to start face to face conversations and learned about different roles in Tech. I also learned in this event about SheTech and Found a love for leadership. Febuary 4th-6th, I got to attend my first Silicon Slopes Summit. This was truly life changing for me! This was a big learning curve on how to walk up to people and start a conversation about many different roles in Tech. I got to learn from many guests speakers, learn about companies, step out of my comfort zone, more NetWorking and learn how to learn more! Febuary 24th, I got to attend SheTech as a Mentor and do a Stem TechChallenge with middle and high schools students. I lead a group of 6 girls. This was my first experience ever doing something like this! This taught me how to be a good role model and how to use time management in a TechChallenge. During this time, I got to reconnect with a guest speaker we had in class. She was a HRIS Specalist. I felt really empowered by the end of the TechChallenge, and help me keep pushing in my learning.
